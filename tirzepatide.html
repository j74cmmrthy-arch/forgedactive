TIRZEPATIDE

LY3298176 · Dual Agonist

A dual-receptor agonist targeting both GIP and GLP-1 pathways. The most clinically studied compound in the modern GLP class, with landmark trial data on body weight reduction, glycemic control, and cardiovascular outcomes.

// How It Works

Mechanism of Action

GLP-1 Receptor

Glucagon-Like Peptide 1

GLP-1 agonism slows gastric emptying, stimulates glucose-dependent insulin secretion, and suppresses glucagon release. The clinical result is improved blood sugar regulation, prolonged satiety, and a reduction in appetite, including the quieting of "food noise," the constant mental background chatter around eating that many people don't realize affects them until it's gone.

GIP Receptor

Glucose-Dependent Insulinotropic Peptide

GIP is often called the forgotten incretin. Tirzepatide's dual GIP action enhances insulin sensitivity in adipose tissue and appears to modulate the GLP-1 side effect profile, potentially improving tolerability compared to GLP-1-only compounds. This receptor component is the molecular differentiator from semaglutide.

// Published Research

What the Science Says

SURMOUNT-1 — Phase 3 Obesity Trial

Participants on tirzepatide 15mg achieved a mean body weight reduction of 20.9% at 72 weeks, the highest weight loss recorded in a non-surgical pharmacological obesity trial at the time of publication. Over 50% of participants on the highest dose achieved at least 20% weight loss. The placebo group lost 3.1%.

Authors: Jastreboff AM, Aronne LJ, Ahmad NN, et al.
Title: Tirzepatide Once Weekly for the Treatment of Obesity
Journal: New England Journal of Medicine
Year: 2022  |  PMID: 35658024
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2206038

SURPASS-2 — Head-to-Head vs. Semaglutide

In the only head-to-head Phase 3 trial against semaglutide 1mg, all tirzepatide doses produced significantly greater HbA1c reductions and weight loss. The 15mg dose reduced HbA1c by 2.46% versus 1.86% for semaglutide, and body weight by 11.2kg versus 5.7kg.

Authors: Frías JP, Davies MJ, Rosenstock J, et al.
Title: Tirzepatide versus Semaglutide Once Weekly in Patients with Type 2 Diabetes
Journal: New England Journal of Medicine
Year: 2021  |  PMID: 34170647
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2107519

Cardiovascular Outcomes — Class Reference

SELECT trial data on semaglutide established significant cardiovascular event reduction for the GLP-1 class in people with obesity and established cardiovascular disease. Ongoing tirzepatide cardiovascular outcome trials are expected to build on this class-level finding.

Authors: Lincoff AM, Brown-Frandsen K, Colhoun HM, et al.
Title: Semaglutide and Cardiovascular Outcomes in Obesity without Diabetes
Journal: New England Journal of Medicine
Year: 2023  |  PMID: 37952131
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa2307563

// Friends and Family Experience

What They Reported

Weeks 1–4

The Adjustment

Appetite suppression was noticeable within the first week for most. Nausea was the dominant side effect early, particularly in weeks one through three. Eating smaller, protein-forward meals more frequently helped significantly. Energy was variable as overall caloric intake dropped.

Weeks 4–12

Finding the Rhythm

Nausea largely resolved by week six for most people. Appetite regulation felt more natural, less like white-knuckling cravings and more like genuinely not being hungry. Visual body composition changes became apparent around weeks eight to ten.

Most Notable

The Food Noise Effect

The most consistently reported surprise was the quieting of "food noise," the constant mental background chatter about eating. Many people don't realize how much cognitive bandwidth this occupies until it's gone. This is one of the most frequently described quality-of-life improvements across the GLP class.

Watchpoints

What to Monitor

Protein intake needs active attention since overall food volume drops significantly. Hydration is critical and frequently underestimated. Working with a provider to monitor lipase, amylase, and liver enzymes on longer protocols is worth doing.

// Common Questions

Tirzepatide FAQ

Tirzepatide targets two receptors: GIP and GLP-1. Retatrutide targets three: GIP, GLP-1, and glucagon. The addition of glucagon receptor agonism in retatrutide increases resting energy expenditure and enhances fat mobilization, which is why Phase 2 data showed greater weight reduction with retatrutide. However, tirzepatide is FDA approved with a much larger clinical safety dataset. Retatrutide is still in Phase 3 trials. Both have their place depending on goals, health profile, and what a provider recommends.
Tirzepatide is available in 2.5mg, 5mg, 7.5mg, 10mg, 12.5mg, and 15mg doses. Most protocols start at 2.5mg weekly and titrate up every four weeks based on tolerability. The maximum approved dose is 15mg weekly. Always follow the titration schedule from your prescribing provider.
Gastrointestinal side effects are the most common: nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, and constipation, particularly during dose escalation. These typically improve two to four weeks after stabilizing at a given dose. Slowing the titration schedule is the most effective management strategy. Rare but serious risks include pancreatitis and thyroid C-cell effects observed in rodent models.
Appetite is significantly reduced, so every meal becomes more nutritionally important. Prioritize protein-dense, nutrient-rich foods. Avoid high-fat, greasy meals as they tend to worsen nausea. Staying well hydrated is critical and more commonly overlooked than most people expect.
Most people experience some weight regain after stopping since the appetite-regulating effects are tied to the compound being active. Research consistently shows that sustained use or a maintenance-dose protocol outperforms cycling on and off. Building durable lifestyle habits during the protocol is the best long-term strategy.
