GHK-CU

Glycyl-L-Histidyl-L-Lysine · Copper Complex

A naturally occurring peptide (short protein chain) found in human plasma, saliva, and urine that binds and transports copper to cells. Concentrations decline with age. Research documents its role in collagen production, wound healing, inflammation reduction, and hair follicle stimulation — making it a well-supported complement to GLP protocols.

// How It Works

Mechanism of Action

Copper Transport

Getting Copper Where It Needs to Go

GHK-Cu works by binding copper ions and delivering them to specific cells and tissues. Copper is essential for collagen cross-linking (the process that makes collagen fibers strong and organized), elastin formation, and antioxidant defense. GHK-Cu acts as a precision delivery system rather than a free copper supplement, which matters because unbound copper can be harmful.

Gene Regulation

Broad Cellular Effects

Research has identified GHK-Cu as a regulator of over 4,000 human genes — including those governing inflammation, DNA repair, antioxidant response, and tissue remodeling. This broad activity is what makes it more than just a topical skin product, and why it is increasingly used as part of systemic wellness protocols.

// Published Research

What the Science Shows

Skin, Collagen, and Dermal Repair

Multiple studies demonstrate GHK-Cu increases collagen synthesis (production of the structural protein that gives skin firmness), stimulates fibroblast proliferation (growth of the cells responsible for making collagen), and measurably improves skin thickness and elasticity. These effects are particularly relevant when significant weight loss changes skin texture and laxity.

Authors: Pickart L, Vasquez-Soltero JM, Margolina A
Title: GHK Peptide as a Natural Modulator of Multiple Cellular Pathways in Skin Regeneration
Journal: BioMed Research International
Year: 2015  |  PMID: 26090436
DOI: 10.1155/2015/648108

Wound Healing and Tissue Repair

GHK-Cu accelerates wound healing by promoting new blood vessel formation and modulating TGF-beta signaling — a pathway that controls whether injured tissue heals with organized structure or forms disorganized scar tissue. It appears to favor the former, supporting cleaner tissue repair.

Authors: Pickart L
Title: The Human Tri-Peptide GHK and Tissue Remodeling
Journal: Journal of Biomaterials Science, Polymer Edition
Year: 2008  |  PMID: 18454942
DOI: 10.1163/156856208784909435

Hair Follicle Stimulation

Studies show GHK-Cu promotes hair follicle enlargement and stimulates growth factor signaling. This is directly relevant for GLP users, as rapid weight loss can trigger telogen effluvium — a form of temporary hair shedding caused by physiological stress (the body prioritizing resources elsewhere during rapid change).

// What to Expect

Week by Week

Based on personal experience. GHK-Cu builds gradually — it is not a fast-acting compound. Results compound over weeks and months.

1–2

Weeks 1–2

Minimal visible changes in the first two weeks for most users. If using subcutaneous injection, you may experience a localized burning or "spicy" sensation at the injection site — this is site-specific, not systemic, and resolves by moving to an area with more fatty tissue (like the dorsogluteal area).

3–4

Weeks 3–4

The first noticeable changes tend to appear here — faster nail and hair growth being the most commonly reported early signal. Skin may begin to feel more hydrated and resilient. These changes can be subtle but become a reliable early indicator that the compound is working.

6–8

Weeks 6–8

Skin firmness and texture improvements become more apparent. Workout recovery may feel slightly faster. Hair quality and density continue improving. The cumulative collagen remodeling effects are building in the background even when surface changes seem to plateau.

8+

Week 8 and Beyond

Most people complete a cycle of 2 months and take a 1-month break before the next cycle. The break allows the body to consolidate gains without desensitization. Results from the first cycle inform how to approach the next one.

// Experience and Protocol

How It Was Used

Personal Experience

My Protocol

I used 2mg nightly via subcutaneous (subQ) injection — into the fatty layer under the skin, not into muscle. Timing the injection before bed was intentional: collagen repair and signaling activity ramps up during sleep, so the overnight window was a logical choice for a compound that supports those processes.


I initially injected into the love handles (the soft tissue at the sides of the lower abdomen), but experienced an injection site reaction — a localized spicy, burning sensation at the injection point. I moved to the dorsogluteal area (upper outer buttock, the fatty region rather than the muscle beneath) and had no further issues.


I also supplemented with vitamins and foods known to support collagen production — vitamin C, zinc, and collagen-rich foods — to complement the compound's signaling effects.

Results and Cycle

What I Noticed

Results became apparent around week 3. Hair and nails were visibly growing faster — something I had noticed slowing while on a GLP compound, likely due to reduced overall nutrition intake. Skin felt firmer over time.


The hair and nail changes were a clear signal: GLP protocols reduce how much you eat, which means nutrients that support hair and nail growth can become limited. GHK-Cu appeared to help offset this directly.


I ran two months on, then took a one-month break before considering another cycle. This cycling approach is common in the community and gives the body a reset period.


For the injection site reaction: if you experience that burning sensation at one site, simply move the injection location. It is not harmful — just uncomfortable — and site-specific.
