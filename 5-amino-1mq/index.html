5-AMINO-1MQ

5-Amino-1-Methylquinolinium · NNMT Inhibitor

A small molecule compound that inhibits NNMT (Nicotinamide N-methyltransferase) — an enzyme found in fat tissue that, when overactive, promotes fat storage and suppresses energy metabolism. By blocking NNMT, 5-Amino-1MQ may support fat loss, increase cellular energy, and improve metabolic flexibility.

// How It Works

Mechanism of Action

NNMT Inhibition

Changing How Fat Cells Behave

NNMT (Nicotinamide N-methyltransferase) is an enzyme that is highly active in fat tissue. When NNMT is overexpressed (working too much), fat cells become more metabolically inactive — they store energy efficiently but burn very little. By inhibiting NNMT, 5-Amino-1MQ shifts fat cells toward a more metabolically active state, increasing energy expenditure and reducing fat storage capacity at the cellular level.

NAD+ Pathway

Supporting Cellular Energy

NNMT consumes NAD+ precursors (compounds the body uses to produce NAD+, a molecule essential for energy production in every cell). By reducing NNMT activity, 5-Amino-1MQ preserves more of these precursors for NAD+ synthesis — which supports mitochondrial function (the energy-producing machinery inside cells), cellular repair, and metabolic efficiency.

// Published Research

What the Science Shows

Fat Loss in Preclinical Models

In mouse models of diet-induced obesity, NNMT inhibition with compounds in this class produced significant reductions in fat mass without changes to food intake or lean muscle mass. Treated animals showed increased energy expenditure — meaning they burned more calories at rest — and improved insulin sensitivity compared to controls.

Authors: Neelakantan H, Vance V, Wetzel MD, et al.
Title: Selective and membrane-permeable small molecule inhibitors of nicotinamide N-methyltransferase reverse high fat diet-induced obesity in mice
Journal: Biochemical Pharmacology
Year: 2018  |  PMID: 29499180
DOI: 10.1016/j.bcp.2018.02.036

NNMT and Metabolic Disease

Elevated NNMT expression in fat tissue has been associated with insulin resistance, obesity, and type 2 diabetes in human studies. NNMT inhibition represents a novel target for improving metabolic health by directly altering the behavior of fat cells rather than simply suppressing appetite or increasing activity.

Authors: Kraus D, Yang Q, Kong D, et al.
Title: Nicotinamide N-methyltransferase knockdown protects against diet-induced obesity
Journal: Nature
Year: 2014  |  PMID: 24476821
DOI: 10.1038/nature12845

// What to Expect

Week by Week

Based on personal experience. 5-Amino-1MQ tends to produce noticeable energy effects early, with body composition changes building over the full cycle.

Days 1–3

Many users notice an energy shift within the first few days — a clean, focused alertness before and during workouts without the jitteriness or elevated heart rate associated with stimulants. The comparison to matcha energy is a consistent description across community reports.

Weeks 1–3

Workout quality and endurance improve consistently. Post-workout fatigue reduces — finishing a training session and then being functional for the rest of the day becomes more reliable. Injection site reactions (burning sensation) may appear at leaner sites and resolve by switching location.

Weeks 4–8

Body composition changes begin to accumulate — improved metabolic rate, better fat oxidation during training, and improved insulin sensitivity contribute to gradual changes in how the body looks and performs. These changes are subtle at this stage but directionally consistent.

8+

Week 8 and Beyond

End of a standard 2-month cycle. Cycling off gives the body a reset period. Most community users report that the energy and focus benefits are the most immediately noticeable loss when cycling off, which provides a useful signal about the compound's contribution.

// Experience and Protocol

How It Was Used

Personal Experience

My Protocol

I took 10mg every morning before working out via subcutaneous (subQ) injection — into the fatty layer under the skin, not into muscle. I injected into the dorsogluteal area (upper outer buttock) because the love handles produced a localized burning sensation, similar to what I experienced with GHK-Cu. I deliberately did not overlap injection sites between compounds.


The effect I noticed most was a clean, focused energy — comparable to matcha but without jitteriness or an elevated heart rate. I felt mentally engaged during workouts and was not fatigued afterwards, even on days I had to work a full shift after training.


I used 5-Amino-1MQ for two months and am currently cycling off while transitioning to MOTS-c.

Community Protocol

General Approach

The biohacking community typically uses 5-Amino-1MQ at doses between 5mg and 20mg, most commonly taken in the morning given its energizing effects. It is used both as an injectable and, in some formulations, orally.


Pre-workout timing is the most commonly reported approach, with users noting improved training focus, endurance, and recovery. It is often stacked with NAD+ precursors like NMN or NR to amplify the cellular energy pathway it activates.


Most community protocols cycle the compound — 6 to 8 weeks on, 4 to 6 weeks off — though there is limited long-term human data to establish optimal cycle length. Individual response varies.


Injection site reactions (localized burning) are reported by some users, particularly at leaner injection sites. Moving to areas with more subcutaneous fat resolves this in most cases.
