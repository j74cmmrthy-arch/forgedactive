TESAMORELIN

GHRH Analog · TH9507

A synthetic analog of GHRH (growth hormone-releasing hormone) — the hormone your hypothalamus naturally produces to signal the pituitary gland to release growth hormone (GH). Unlike direct GH injections, tesamorelin works through the body's own feedback system, producing a more physiologically natural GH pulse pattern. FDA approved for HIV-associated lipodystrophy (excess visceral fat accumulation), it is also used in wellness settings for visceral fat reduction, muscle fullness, and body composition.

// How It Works

Mechanism of Action

GHRH Signaling

Stimulating Natural GH Release

Tesamorelin binds to GHRH receptors in the pituitary gland (a small gland at the base of the brain that controls hormone production), triggering the release of growth hormone. Because it works through the body's existing regulatory system — including natural negative feedback loops — it produces pulsatile (pulse-like, rhythmic) GH release rather than a constant flood. This mimics how GH is naturally secreted and reduces the risk of over-suppression of the body's own production.

IGF-1 Pathway

Downstream Effects

The growth hormone released in response to tesamorelin stimulates IGF-1 (insulin-like growth factor 1) production in the liver. IGF-1 is the primary mediator of GH's anabolic (tissue-building) effects — promoting muscle protein synthesis (building of muscle tissue), fat metabolism, and tissue repair. IGF-1 levels are often used as a proxy marker to assess whether a GHRH protocol is working.

// Published Research

What the Science Says

Visceral Fat Reduction — FDA Approval Trials

The pivotal trials supporting tesamorelin's FDA approval demonstrated significant reductions in visceral adipose tissue (VAT — the deep abdominal fat surrounding organs, associated with cardiovascular and metabolic risk) in HIV-positive individuals with lipodystrophy. Participants receiving 2mg daily showed approximately 15–20% reductions in trunk fat compared to placebo over 26 weeks.

Authors: Falutz J, Allas S, Blot K, et al.
Title: Metabolic effects of a growth hormone-releasing factor in patients with HIV
Journal: New England Journal of Medicine
Year: 2007  |  PMID: 17687132
DOI: 10.1056/NEJMoa066418

Muscle Fullness and Body Composition

Studies in both HIV-positive and general populations showed tesamorelin increased lean body mass and reduced fat mass while preserving bone density. Users in wellness contexts consistently report improved muscle fullness — the visual and tactile density of muscle that indicates good hydration and glycogen storage within the tissue.

Authors: Stanley TL, Feldpausch MN, Oh J, et al.
Title: Effect of tesamorelin on visceral fat and liver fat in HIV-infected patients with abdominal fat accumulation
Journal: Journal of Infectious Diseases
Year: 2012  |  PMID: 22291103
DOI: 10.1093/infdis/jir802

Hair, Nails, and Connective Tissue

Growth hormone and IGF-1 both play roles in keratin production (the structural protein that makes up hair and nails), collagen synthesis, and skin thickness. Community users of tesamorelin consistently report improvements in hair thickness, nail growth and strength, and skin quality — consistent with the known downstream effects of GH-mediated IGF-1 elevation.

Authors: Yuen KCJ, Biller BMK, Radovick S, et al.
Title: American Association of Clinical Endocrinologists and American College of Endocrinology Guidelines for Management of Growth Hormone Deficiency in Adults
Journal: Endocrine Practice
Year: 2019  |  PMID: 31368602
DOI: 10.4158/GL-2019-0405

// What to Expect

General Timeline

Based on clinical trial data and community experience. Individual results depend on baseline GH levels, diet, training, and sleep quality (GH is primarily released during deep sleep).

1–2

Weeks 1–2

Sleep quality improvements are often the first thing reported — deeper sleep and more vivid dreams. Mild water retention (slight puffiness, particularly in the hands and face) is common early as GH influences fluid balance and typically resolves after the first few weeks.

3–6

Weeks 3–6

Energy levels and workout recovery begin to improve. Muscle fullness becomes more noticeable, particularly in trained individuals. Skin may appear more supple. Hair and nail growth typically accelerates in this window.

8–12

Weeks 8–12

Visceral fat reduction becomes measurable. Body composition changes — more muscle definition and less abdominal fat — are typically visible by this point in users who are also training and eating adequately.

16+

Week 16 and Beyond

Sustained body composition improvements, continued visceral fat reduction, and cumulative connective tissue and skin benefits with ongoing use. Most clinical protocols run 26 weeks for measurable fat reduction outcomes.

// Experience and Protocol

How It Is Used

Personal insights coming soon. In the meantime, here is how the broader wellness community approaches tesamorelin.

Community Protocol

Common Approach

  • Dose: 1–2mg subcutaneous (subQ) injection daily
  • Timing: Before bed to align with the body's natural overnight GH pulse — the largest GH release of the day occurs during deep sleep
  • Injection site: Abdomen, rotating sites to avoid tissue buildup
  • Cycle: Typically 3–6 months on, followed by a break to prevent desensitization of GHRH receptors
  • Monitoring: IGF-1 blood levels checked at baseline and 6–8 weeks in to assess response and avoid over-elevation
  • Stack: Often used with GHRP peptides (like ipamorelin) to amplify the GH pulse, or with GHK-Cu for complementary tissue support
Key Considerations

What to Know Before Starting

  • Sleep quality directly determines how much benefit you get — GH is released during deep sleep stages, so optimizing sleep is not optional here
  • Alcohol close to bedtime significantly blunts GH release and reduces the effectiveness of the protocol
  • Fasting for 2–3 hours before the injection improves GH pulse amplitude (the height of the GH spike)
  • Mild side effects like water retention, joint aches (from fluid shifts), and tingling (carpal tunnel-like sensation) are common early and typically resolve
  • Not recommended for people with active cancer, as GH can promote cell growth broadly
  • IGF-1 monitoring is important — chronically elevated IGF-1 carries its own risk profile

Tesamorelin FAQ

Direct GH injections bypass the pituitary entirely and deliver a constant level of GH, which can suppress the pituitary's own production over time and disrupt natural feedback loops. Tesamorelin stimulates the pituitary to release GH in its natural pulsatile pattern, preserving feedback regulation. This is generally considered a safer and more physiologically appropriate approach, particularly for long-term use.
