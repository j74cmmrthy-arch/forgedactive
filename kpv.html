KPV

Lysine-Proline-Valine · Alpha-MSH Fragment

A tripeptide derived from the C-terminus of alpha-melanocyte stimulating hormone, known primarily for its potent anti-inflammatory properties. Research covers inflammatory bowel conditions, skin inflammation, wound healing, and systemic inflammation reduction, making it particularly relevant for conditions driven by chronic inflammatory signaling.

// How It Works

Mechanism of Action

MC1R Agonism

Melanocortin Pathway

KPV is derived from the C-terminal sequence of alpha-MSH and exerts its anti-inflammatory effects primarily through the melanocortin-1 receptor (MC1R). This pathway downregulates NF-kB signaling, one of the central drivers of inflammatory cascades throughout the body, including in gut epithelium and skin.

Gut and Skin

Where It Acts

KPV shows particular activity in gut epithelial tissue, making it relevant to inflammatory bowel conditions, and in skin, where it modulates keratinocyte and immune cell inflammatory signaling. Its small tripeptide structure allows it to penetrate tissues effectively and act at the site of inflammation directly.

// Published Research

What the Science Shows

Gut Inflammation and Colitis

Studies in murine colitis models found KPV significantly reduced inflammatory markers including TNF-alpha, IL-6, and NF-kB activity in gut epithelial tissue. Oral administration showed bioactivity, and the peptide demonstrated protective effects on the intestinal mucosal barrier during inflammatory challenge.

Authors: Dalmasso G, Charrier-Hisamuddin L, Nguyen HT, et al.
Title: PepT1-mediated tripeptide KPV uptake reduces intestinal inflammation
Journal: Gastroenterology
Year: 2008  |  PMID: 18082524
DOI: 10.1053/j.gastro.2007.10.026

Skin Inflammation and Wound Healing

KPV has been studied for its ability to reduce skin inflammation and accelerate wound healing. Topical application reduced inflammatory cytokine expression in skin models and promoted organized healing responses. Its MC1R-mediated activity in keratinocytes makes it a candidate for inflammatory skin conditions including eczema and psoriasis.

Authors: Brzoska T, Luger TA, Maaser C, et al.
Title: Alpha-melanocyte-stimulating hormone and related tripeptides: biochemistry, antiinflammatory and protective effects in vitro and in vivo, and future perspectives for the treatment of immune-mediated inflammatory diseases
Journal: Endocrine Reviews
Year: 2008  |  PMID: 18349283
DOI: 10.1210/er.2007-0027
